Applications of the Results

The evaluation results show that both models can be used in practical SMS classification tasks, especially for filtering and organizing incoming messages based on their content and sender.

  • Spam detection: Messages predicted as spam can be flagged early to help users avoid scams, phishing attempts, and unwanted promotions.
  • Automatic SMS categorization: The models can sort messages into categories such as ads, OTP, notifications, government messages, and spam for easier inbox management.
  • Fraud awareness support: Since the system considers both sender and message content, it can help identify suspicious sender-message combinations that may look legitimate at first glance.
  • Priority message handling: Important messages such as OTPs and trusted notifications can be separated from low-priority or risky messages.
  • Decision support for users: Confidence scores and interpretations can help users understand why a message may be risky or safe before taking action.
  • Integration into mobile or web tools: These results suggest the models can be embedded into messaging dashboards, security tools, or SMS screening applications.
  • Model comparison for deployment: Since both the Ensemble and Neural Network are shown separately, developers can compare which model is more stable, accurate, and practical for real-world use.
